Private Limited Company Registration in Bangalore

Establishing a Private Limited Company in India offers numerous advantages, such as limited liability protection, enhanced credibility, and ease of raising capital. However, it requires careful planning and compliance with legal requirements to ensure smooth incorporation and long-term success.

At Balakrishna and Co., Chartered Accountants, we specialize in guiding businesses through the incorporation process efficiently and seamlessly.

Overview of Private Limited Company Registration

Minimum Number of Shareholders and Directors

A Private Limited Company must have at least two shareholders and two directors, with at least one director being a resident Indian. Shareholders can also act as directors, simplifying the structure for smaller businesses.

Choosing the Company Name

Choosing an appropriate company name is a critical step in the incorporation process. The name should be unique, comply with legal requirements, and reflect the company’s objectives. It should also avoid similarity with existing companies or trademarks.

Obtaining Digital Signature Certificates (DSC)

Digital Signature Certificates are required for all directors to enable secure online submission of incorporation documents.

Obtaining Director Identification Number (DIN)

Each proposed director must obtain a Director Identification Number through the Ministry of Corporate Affairs. This is a mandatory identification requirement for company directors.

Drafting MoA and AoA

The Memorandum of Association and Articles of Association define the company’s objectives, internal rules, and governance structure. These documents must be prepared carefully to ensure legal compliance.

Filing for Incorporation

Once the name is approved, the incorporation application is filed with the Registrar of Companies along with the required documents, including director details, shareholder details, and proof of registered office address.

Issuance of Incorporation Certificate

After verification of the submitted documents, the Registrar of Companies issues the Incorporation Certificate along with the Corporate Identity Number, formally establishing the company.

Post-Incorporation Steps

After incorporation, the company must complete important initial compliance activities such as issuing share certificates, updating the registered office if required, filing for commencement of business, conducting the first board meeting, and appointing the first auditor.

Documents Required for Company Registration

  • Aadhaar (self-attested copy)
  • PAN (self-attested copy)
  • Passport, if available
  • Recent bank statement containing name and full address
  • Passport-size photograph
  • Email ID and mobile number
  • Rental agreement or sale deed for registered office
  • No Objection Certificate from the landlord, if applicable
  • Utility bill of the registered office address, not older than two months
  • Proposed paid-up capital details
  • Capital sharing details among promoters
  • Business objective of the proposed company
  • At least three suggested company names
  • Registered office address
  • Existing DIN details, if available
